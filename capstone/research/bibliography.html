Davis Centenial Seal

The website is a detailed description and analysis of the Davis Centennial Seal, which was installed in 2021 to commemorate the 100th anniversary of Davis city's incorporation. The seal, designed by local artist Susan Shelton, represents the history, culture, and aspirations of Davis city through a complex array of imagery and symbolism. The source is extremely valuable for anyone wishing to understand the deep symbolism behind the seal, as it breaks down each component of the seal's design in a methodical manner. The author concludes that the seal is not just a historical representation, but a symbol of community, leadership, innovation, and more. This detailed explanation of the seal can provide a deeper appreciation of its intricate symbolism and the story of Davis city.

Outer ring of the seal

This source describes the symbolic meaning of the outer ring of the Davis Centennial Seal, focusing on the depiction of Putah Creek's native flora and fauna. The narrative underscores the historical significance of the creek, its decline due to human alteration, and its revival due to citizen-led conservation efforts. This source is useful for understanding the underlying meanings and symbolism in the seal's design. The author concludes by linking the creek's restoration with the ideals of cooperation, engagement, vision, and leadership. I concur with this conclusion, recognizing the seal as a profound tribute to the community's shared values and environmental stewardship.
